Section 03
UX Improvements — Quick Wins
These are UI/UX changes that will have an immediate impact on usability without requiring major backend work.
01
Add a Barcode / SKU Scan Input to the Sales Screen
A text field at the top of the product area that accepts keyboard input from a USB barcode scanner (which presents itself as a keyboard). When a SKU is typed/scanned and Enter is pressed, the product is instantly added to the cart. This is how every real POS terminal works and is critical for speed at the counter. No camera needed — just a text field that fires on Enter.
Low effort
02
Cash Tendered & Change Calculator on Checkout
When "Cash" is selected as payment method, show a "Cash received: R___" input. Auto-calculate and display change due: "Change: R___". This is a basic POS requirement that prevents cashier errors and speeds up cash transactions. Currently the system completes a sale without this step.
Low effort
03
Quantity Input on Product Selection
When a product is tapped/clicked, show a quantity selector (or allow long-press / right-click to enter quantity directly). A cashier selling 12 units of the same item should not have to click 12 times. Add a qty input in the cart row alongside the item name.
Low effort
04
Sample Data in Reports During Onboarding
New accounts see a completely blank Reports screen — all R0.00 with an empty chart. This kills first impressions. Show sample/demo data with a "This is example data — your real stats will appear here once you start selling" banner. Let new users see what the reports WILL look like, so they understand the value before they've made a single sale.
Low effort
05
Product Images in Inventory & Sales Screen
Allow users to upload a product image (or auto-fetch from a product name lookup). Display as a thumbnail in the product list and as a card/tile in the sales screen. Visual product selection is significantly faster than name-reading, especially for staff who didn't set up the product database themselves.
Medium effort
06
Overdue Invoice Highlight + Aging Indicator
In the Invoices list, add a red "Overdue" badge next to any invoice where terms have passed and it's still unpaid. Show a subtle aging tag: "7 days overdue", "31 days overdue". Add a filter for "Overdue Only". This single feature reduces debtor days significantly and is the most-requested feature among SA service businesses.
Low effort
07
"Hold Sale" / Park Sale Functionality
Allow a cashier to park an in-progress sale (save it as a named hold) and start a new transaction. This handles the real-world scenario of a customer running to get something while another customer is waiting. A "Parked Sales" indicator in the header restores held transactions.
Medium effort
08
Add Friction to "Delete Account" in Danger Zone
The Delete Account button is far too accessible. Add a multi-step confirmation: (1) warning modal explaining consequences, (2) type the word "DELETE" to confirm, (3) a 24-hour cancellation period where data is soft-deleted first. This prevents accidental deletion and gives unhappy customers a cooling-off window.
Low effort
09
Empty State Improvements Across All Modules
Open Accounts, Purchase Orders, and Reports all show minimal empty states. Each should have: a relevant illustration, a single clear action button ("Create your first PO"), and 1-sentence benefit copy. Good empty states are onboarding — they teach users what the feature does and how to start. Currently they're dead ends.
Low effort
10
Customer Purchase History Panel
When viewing a customer record, show a scrollable transaction history: date, items, amount, payment method. Currently only total spend is shown. This lets business owners answer "what did Frikkie buy last time?" in seconds. Combine with a "Send Statement" button to generate a PDF of all transactions in a date range.
Medium effort
11
Sales Screen Layout: Add Category Filter Tabs
When a business has 50+ products, the current vertical list is slow to navigate. Add horizontal category filter pills/tabs above the product list (e.g., "All | Drinks | Food | Hardware"). Tapping a category instantly filters. This is the standard for modern POS interfaces (Square, Lightspeed, iKhokha all do this).
Medium effort
12
Replace Gmail Support Email with Branded Address
The billing screen shows "sofwarebystorm@gmail.com" (also note the typo: "sofw" not "soft"). This immediately undermines enterprise trust. Set up support@stormsoftware.co.za — costs nothing, takes 10 minutes in Google Workspace or Zoho Mail, and makes a significant perception difference when businesses see it on their billing page.
Low effort
13
Keyboard Shortcut Layer for Power Users
Add keyboard shortcuts for common actions: F1 = New Sale, F2 = Search Products, F3 = Complete Sale, Esc = Cancel, Tab = cycle cart items. Show a cheat-sheet in Settings. This turns Storm POS from a web app into something that feels like professional POS software for high-volume retailers.
Medium effort
14
Onboarding Checklist Progress Bar
The "Complete your account setup" banner at the top of Settings is good but static. Replace it with a visual progress checklist: ✓ Add your logo, ✓ Add your first product, □ Run a test sale, □ Connect payment method, □ Invite a staff member. Each step ticked off reveals the next. Gamified onboarding dramatically improves 7-day activation rates.
Low effort

Create multiple user accounts with role-based access: Cashier (sell only), Manager (reports + discounts), Admin (full access). Cashiers cannot see cost prices or delete records. Critical for any business with employees.

A single account managing multiple store locations. Separate inventory per branch, consolidated reporting across all branches, transfer stock between branches. Enables franchise and chain expansion — your biggest growth market.

Section 05
Billing Model Analysis
Observations on the current pricing model and recommendations for improvement.

Current Model: Percentage + Per-Invoice Fee

The Percentage Plan charges 0.5% of gross revenue + R0.50 per invoice generated. The Flat Rate Plan charges R1.00 per POS transaction + R0.50 per invoice. This is a usage-based model — businesses pay proportionally to their revenue. This is fair and appropriate for early-stage SA SMEs, but it has significant strategic implications:

✅ What works well
Zero barrier to entry
No fixed monthly fee means a business doing R0 in a slow month pays R0. This removes the biggest objection for SA SMEs who fear committing to a subscription. Excellent for word-of-mouth growth.
✅ What works well
Aligned incentives
Storm earns more as the business earns more. This is the same model as Shopify Payments, Square, and Yoco. Customers feel the fee is "fair" because it scales with their success rather than being a fixed overhead.
⚠️ Risk to address
High-volume businesses will outgrow % pricing
A business doing R200k/month pays R1,000/month at 0.5%. They'll quickly calculate this and switch to Flat Rate — or leave for a fixed-price competitor. Consider a third tier: a flat R399–R799/month with unlimited transactions for established businesses.
⚠️ Risk to address
Invoice fee creates friction for invoice-heavy users
A business generating 100 invoices/month pays R50 just in invoice fees on top of the revenue percentage. This isn't communicated clearly on the marketing page. Invoice-heavy users (agencies, contractors) may feel penalised. Consider bundling X free invoices per tier.
💡 Recommendation
Add a third "Scale" tier
Consider: Starter (0.5% + R0.50/inv), Growth (R599/month flat, 200 invoices included), Scale (R999/month, unlimited, multi-location, priority support). This captures different segments and increases ARPU as businesses grow.
💡 Recommendation
Disclose fees on the marketing page
The /pos page references "R799/mo" in site metadata but the actual model is usage-based with no fixed monthly fee. This mismatch creates trust issues when users see their real billing. Update the marketing page to show the actual pricing model clearly.
